What is a new construction loan?

A new construction loan is short-term financing for building a property from the ground up. It typically covers land acquisition, vertical construction, and an interest reserve on a single loan, with construction funds disbursed via draws as the build progresses through inspection milestones. Once construction is complete, the loan is paid off through a sale of the finished property or refinanced into long-term financing.

SLA borrowers commonly build a rental property using a New Construction loan, then refinance it into a long-term DSCR loan at completion once the property is stabilized.

New Construction FAQ

Common questions about New Construction loans.

What is a new construction loan?

A new construction loan is short-term financing for building a property from the ground up. It typically funds land acquisition, vertical construction, and an interest reserve on a single loan, with construction funds disbursed via draws as the build progresses. Once construction is complete, the loan is paid off through a sale or refinanced into long-term product.

How much of the project does SLA Capital finance?

SLA Capital finances up to 85% of the land purchase plus 85% of construction costs. Loans range from $100,000 to $7,500,000.

Does the land need to be permitted to qualify?

No. SLA Capital does not require the land to be permitted or warranted before loan approval. Get under contract and start underwriting in parallel with permitting.

How is interest calculated on the loan?

Non-Dutch interest — borrowers pay interest only on funds that have been drawn, not on the undrawn balance of the construction budget. This keeps carrying costs down during early build phases when most of the loan hasn't been disbursed yet.

What are the term options?

18-month and 24-month terms available. Extensions can be added on active projects for reasonable extension fees.

What are New Construction loan rates?

Rates start from 10%. Every rate is transparent and up-front — no bait-and-switch at the closing table.
