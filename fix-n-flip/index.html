What is a Fix & Flip loan?

A Fix and Flip loan is short-term bridge financing used to purchase and rehabilitate a distressed property before selling or refinancing it. Loan size is anchored to the property's after-repair value (ARV) — not just its current condition — so investors can finance both the acquisition and the renovation on a single loan. Rehab dollars are disbursed via draws as work is completed and inspected.

Most SLA borrowers use a Fix & Flip loan to acquire and rehab, then either sell for profit or refinance into a long-term DSCR loan once the property is stabilized and rented.

Fix & Flip FAQ

Common questions about Fix & Flip loans.

What is a Fix and Flip loan?

A Fix and Flip loan is a short-term bridge loan used to purchase and rehabilitate an investment property before selling or refinancing. Loan size is sized off the after-repair value (ARV), and rehab costs are financed via draws disbursed as work is completed.

How fast can SLA Capital close a Fix & Flip loan?

As little as 72 hours from application to funding when the title is clear and the file is complete. Average close across all Fix & Flip loans is 7–10 days.

How much of the rehab budget does SLA finance?

SLA Capital finances 100% of rehab costs. Funds are disbursed via draws after inspection of completed work. Photo-and-invoice draw requests run through our platform for same-day approvals.

What's the maximum leverage on a Fix & Flip loan?

Up to 100% loan-to-cost (LTC) for premier repeat borrowers. First-time borrowers typically qualify for lower LTC based on experience and deal strength.

What are the rates and points?

Rates range 9.5% to 12%. Origination points range 1 to 4. Every quote is transparent and up-front — the rate you're quoted is the rate you close at.

Any application or appraisal fees?

No. Zero application fees, zero appraisal fees, no hidden costs. What you're quoted is what you close at.
