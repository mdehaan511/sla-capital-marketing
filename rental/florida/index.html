Florida rental market

Why Florida is a core market for investor lending.

Florida is one of the deepest single-family rental markets in the country and a top-three geography for SLA Capital by loan volume. Three structural reasons investors keep buying Florida:

No state income tax

Rental income and capital gains from Florida properties are not subject to state income tax — a meaningful yield boost over the hold period.

Sustained population inflow

Florida continues to attract high volumes of domestic migration — retirees, remote workers, and families relocating from higher-tax states. Rental demand stays deep across every metro.

STR + LTR flexibility

Metros like Orlando, Miami, and the Gulf Coast have active short-term rental markets alongside long-term rentals — Florida investors often run mixed strategies on the same portfolio.

Florida DSCR FAQ

Common questions about DSCR loans in Florida.

Does SLA Capital lend on rental properties in Florida?

Yes. SLA Capital funds DSCR rental loans throughout Florida — Miami, Tampa, Orlando, Jacksonville, Fort Lauderdale, and every other Florida metro. Rates from 5.75%, up to 80% LTV.

What DSCR loan rates are available in Florida?

Florida DSCR loans start at 5.75% on a 30-year fixed. Rates are priced off the 5-year Treasury with transparent up-front pricing — the rate you're quoted is the rate you close at.

What Florida metros does SLA Capital serve?

Every Florida metro. High-volume markets: Miami-Dade, Tampa Bay, Orlando, Jacksonville, Fort Lauderdale, Cape Coral–Fort Myers, and Sarasota. Panhandle and secondary markets welcome — send us any Florida property.

Is Florida a good state for rental property investors?

Florida is one of the largest single-family rental markets in the U.S. No state income tax, sustained population inflow, and strong demand for both long-term and short-term rentals. Florida landlord-tenant law is generally landlord-friendly at the state level.

Does SLA finance short-term / vacation rentals in Florida?

Yes. Our DSCR program supports short-term rental income (Airbnb / VRBO) on Florida properties where local zoning permits STR use. Rental income can be qualified via a market rent appraisal or documented STR income history depending on scenario.
